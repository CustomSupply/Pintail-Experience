# Pintail Experience — Background Brief

*Source: voice chat with Claude, 2026-05-21. This document captures the vision Isaac described so it can serve as the starting context for all Pintail Experience work in Cowork.*

---

## The business in one paragraph

A friend of Isaac's owns **Pintail Goods**, a small side-gig brand making high-quality leather and other goods for waterfowl hunters. He's pivoting to **Pintail Experiences** — a curated, faith-based hunting-retreat company. Goods will still be made, but the new core product is multi-day, all-inclusive hunting trips at premium locations across the U.S., wrapped in intentional Christian-faith mentorship and relational discipleship. The hunt is the vehicle; the transformation, deeper relationships, and faith-rooted personal growth are the actual product.

## Founder context

- He's currently in a "really cush" sales job and is weighing whether to leave it to chase this dream full-time.
- His top concern is **protecting his family** financially through the transition — he wants the business case to be airtight before he jumps.
- He already has strong industry relationships: hunting outfitters, dog handlers, and the operational network needed to run trips. The hunt-execution side is dialed in.
- His **father-in-law** holds a Master's in Theology and a PhD, and will co-author the curriculum side of the experience.

## The experience model

- **Format:** 2-, 3-, and 5-day curated retreats
- **Locations:** Some of the best hunting destinations in the country
- **Hunting focus:** Primarily quail and duck, but flexible — the model works for most hunting types
- **Possible trip themes:**
  - Father-son
  - Father-daughter
  - Corporate executive retreats
  - Church men's groups
  - Other targeted cohorts as opportunities arise
- **Curriculum thread:** Christian, faith-based mentorship aimed at building men up — encouraging them to step into the roles God designed for them, lead themselves, lead their families, and live with intention
- **Why hunting as the wrapper:** It's a natural icebreaker that puts men at ease in a "manly" environment, which lowers the guard and opens space for vulnerability, honest conversation, and real bonding
- **What attendees walk away with:** Feeling changed, encouraged, and with deeper, more intentional relationships with the people they brought along

## Where the friend needs help (the gap)

He's strong on logistics and pricing, weak on **formal business rigor**. Specifically:

- A real **business plan** — not just numbers in his head, but a document
- **Financial modeling:** break-even, revenue projections, profit per trip, scenarios
- **Operating agreement** — the legal/structural backbone
- **Mission, vision, values** — formalized
- Anything that helps him feel confident the leap is safe for his family

He has good intuition on pricing per trip and what his margin should look like — that's a starting point to build the rest from.

## The software vision (Isaac's piece)

Isaac wants to build the friend a proprietary **all-in-one experience platform** — effectively a CRM + ERP + client portal combined into one branded system. Key properties:

**Internal side (operator):**

- Manages trips, itineraries, vendors, and clients
- Sends notifications and trip invites
- Tracks everything except invoicing/payments (those stay external for now)

**Client-facing portal:**

- View itineraries for upcoming trips
- Browse trip info, destination details, vendor info
- Receive notifications from the operator

**Vendor side:**

- Vendors who partner on trips get exposure inside the portal — a built-in advertisement channel for them, which becomes part of why they want to work with Pintail

**Strategic logic:** Nobody in the faith-based hunting-experience niche has anything like this. The platform becomes part of the brand and a real moat — it elevates the experience beyond what any competitor can offer with off-the-shelf tools.

## Research scope Isaac wants to run

Two parallel tracks:

1. **Market / business research:**
   - What companies operate in this space (faith-based hunting, premium hunting experiences, curated retreat companies)?
   - How extensive are those businesses?
   - Is there existing software purpose-built for this niche?
   - Where are the gaps a Pintail-built platform could fill?

2. **Software / tooling research:**
   - What CRM and ERP tools exist that could be customized vs. built from scratch?
   - What can be borrowed in pattern, even if not used directly?

A clean, well-organized summary document (this file) that captures the vision in a form the friend can read and digest easily — the basis for all downstream business-plan, financial, and software work.
